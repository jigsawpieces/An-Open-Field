Sparrows, starlings, buzzards.
Bridge, bike, owl. Kite, and
caterpillars, hazel, eggs. Cityscape
and buildings and concrete. The air
and the light. Scrub, gorse, oak. Ice-
creams, windmills. Grey, sideways
rain and the grey flat sea. Squirrels,
sledges, sandwiches. A broken nose.
Ancient ash and great wide skies.

Blackberries, capes. A butterfly.
Duck, pink, sisters, school and snow.
Robins. An otter’s tail, singing,
tingle, mackerel, blight. A man diving
in, a kingfisher there, once.

A hawk, yellow, seals, dogs, squalls.
A big sheet of polythene, the cutting,
deer and toads. Rose gardens, a
causeway, curlew, clouds. Geese and
sheep, silver. Plane trees, kites.

A woman swimming right down the
middle of the river in her wetsuit. The
moon. Storm. A mountain, lake and
moorhen, red. Island, mist, fox,
bench, grass.

That’s the Flying Fox, that doesn’t fly
anymore. Behind that hedge, is a boules
court and tennis courts.

A path you can cycle around, as you learn
to ride your bike.

Here used to be a café over here.

It's is where they keep the canoes and
those little dingy boats.

It’s a good size to do a walk around

Red brick, light brick
the sea, the downs

My mum had a shop and we lived above
the shop

I used to look out the window at
everyone at the bus station

Over this one connecting bridge, there’s
a mill there and a funny little network of
places.

My Gran used to live the other side and
from her house, you used to be able to
walk

the horizon

something you rarely see in the city.

Where the sky joins the land

My friends and I used to cycle around.
There were lots of fields, it could go on
forever, you could keep going and going
and going. You didn’t have to be home
for a time

You didn’t really get a sense of tide.
more like a pond than a sea.

It’s probably identical

 

All the pigeons hang out here

Rain. A very odd honking sound,

Where we’d feed all the ducks
The water is always calm
There are not very many people
Usually, there’s an old person
The p l a n e s, the odd train wandering
by, a lot of pigeons

My kids always wanted to get across to
the island

Buggies, scooters, lollies and
Ice-Cream in the summer

Funny trees without their trunks,
Very good climbing,
Easy to access when you’re little

The smell of wet, damp, grass
